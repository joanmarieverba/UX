About

Hello, I’m Joan Marie Verba.

I believe that the goal of web design is to make applications/web pages that present users with the information they want in the style they prefer. I find this both challenging and exciting, and is one of the reasons I find web design so much fun.

I’m fluent in HTML and CSS, which I use for styling web pages. I’m also experienced in creating and adapting graphics using applications such as Photoshop and InDesign. Whenever appropriate, I enjoy putting together animations and videos to add to the user experience.

I invite you to check out what I’ve put together.

Foundations of User Experience (UX) Design

Grade: 93.54

This module covered the basic concepts of UX design, including:

  • Foundational concepts of UX design: user-centered design, the design processs, accessibiity, and equity-focused design
  • Characteristics of great user experience design
  • Common job responsibilities of entry-level UX designers
  • Design sprints
  • The importance of an online portfolio

Start the UX Design Process

Grade: 90.16

This module covered the concepts of Emphasize, Define, and Ideate as they relate to UX Design.

  • Learn common UX research methods
  • Use empathy to understand user needs and pain points
  • Put together empathy maps, personas, user stories, and user journey maps
  • Develop problem statements
  • Come up with possible solutions
  • Conduct competitive audits
  • Identify and account for biases
  • Begin designing a UX app

Build Wireframes and Low-Fidelity Prototypes

Grade: 94.44

This module covered the basics of wireframes.

  • Formulate a goal statement
  • Draw storyboards: big picture and close-up
  • Understand the difference between low-fidelity and high-fidelity design
  • Apply the principles of information architecture to organize a mobile app.
  • Draw paper wireframes and build a paper prototype
  • Create digital low-fidelity wireframes using Figma
  • Recognize implicit bias and deceptive patterns in design

Conduct UX Research and Test Early Concepts

Grade: 100%

This module covered the details of UX research.

  • Plan a UX research study, including the project background, research goals, research questions, Key Performance Indicators, methodology, participants, and script
  • Conduct a usability study
  • Take notes during a usability study
  • Create affinity diagrams to group and analyze data
  • Synthesize observations from research and come up with insights
  • Develop persuasive presentation skills to share research insights
  • Modify low-fidelity designs based on research insights
